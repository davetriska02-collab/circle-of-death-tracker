# Staff Privacy Notice
## IT Slowness Tracker

| | |
|---|---|
| **Version** | 1.0 |
| **Date** | May 2026 |
| **Applies to** | All clinical and administrative staff at Cranleigh Surgery and Guildowns Group Practice |

---

## Who is collecting your data?

Your data is collected and used jointly by:

- **Cranleigh Surgery**, Cranleigh, Surrey
- **Guildowns Group Practice**, Guildford, Surrey

Both practices are joint data controllers for data collected through this tool. If you have any questions or want to exercise your rights, contact the practice manager at the site where you work.

---

## What data is collected, and why?

When you log an IT session using the IT Slowness Tracker, the following information is recorded:

| Data | Why it is collected |
|---|---|
| **Site** (Cranleigh Surgery or Guildowns Group Practice) | To identify which site is experiencing IT problems |
| **Role category** (GP / Nurse / HCA / Receptionist / Admin / Other) | To identify whether certain staff groups are disproportionately affected |
| **Session type** (Duty / Normal / Admin) | To correlate IT problems with workload type |
| **Session start and end time** | To calculate total time lost and to correlate with system logs |
| **Number and duration of IT freeze incidents** | To quantify the time impact of IT problems |
| **Optional free-text note per incident** | To describe the nature of each freeze (e.g. "EMIS froze on patient summary screen") |
| **Optional session narrative** | To provide additional context about the session as a whole |

**What is NOT collected:** Your name, staff number, NHS number, patient names, patient NHS numbers, or any other patient identifiers.

---

## What is the lawful basis?

Processing is carried out under **UK GDPR Article 6(1)(e) — public task**. Monitoring the performance of clinical IT systems is necessary for the delivery of NHS primary care services and falls within the practices' mandate as NHS providers.

No special category data (Article 9) is processed.

---

## Who can see your data?

Session records are stored as entries in a **private** GitHub repository. Access is limited to:

- Practice partners and practice managers at Cranleigh Surgery and Guildowns Group Practice who hold explicit access to that repository

Aggregated, anonymised summaries (e.g. "X hours lost to IT incidents in April 2026") may be shared with the **Integrated Care Board (ICB)** to support procurement and service-improvement decisions. Individual session records are not shared with the ICB.

---

## Sub-processors

The tool uses the following third-party services to store and transmit your data:

| Service | Purpose | Location |
|---|---|---|
| **GitHub (Microsoft Ireland Operations Ltd)** | Stores session records | EEA / UK (adequacy) |
| **Cloudflare, Inc.** | Transmits submissions securely | EU / UK data centres |

Both hold ISO 27001 certification and operate under UK GDPR-compliant data processing agreements.

---

## How long is your data kept?

Session records are retained for **12 months** from the date of the session, then deleted. The practice manager or nominated IG lead reviews and deletes records on a rolling basis.

---

## Your rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the right to:

| Right | What this means |
|---|---|
| **Access** | Request a copy of your session records |
| **Erasure** | Ask for your records to be deleted |
| **Rectification** | Ask for inaccurate records to be corrected |
| **Objection** | Object to processing of your data |
| **Restriction** | Ask for processing to be restricted while a query is resolved |

Because no names are recorded, your records are identified by session date/time and role. To exercise any of these rights, contact the **practice manager at your site**.

You also have the right to raise a concern with the **Information Commissioner's Office (ICO)**:
- Website: ico.org.uk
- Helpline: 0303 123 1113

---

## Free-text notes — guidance for staff

If you use the optional note fields, please keep notes limited to descriptions of IT problems only (e.g. "EMIS froze when opening pathology results"). **Do not include:**

- Patient names
- Patient dates of birth
- NHS numbers or any other patient identifier
- Staff names

The app will automatically block any entry that contains an NHS number.

---

## Changes to this notice

This notice will be updated if the tool or the way data is processed changes materially. The current version is always available in the IT Slowness Tracker repository.
